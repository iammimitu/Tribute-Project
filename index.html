Rebecca Lee Crumpler

Rebecca's Life in Timeline:

  • 1831- Crumpler was born Rebecca David in Christiana, Delaware and grew up in Pennsylvania where she was raised by her aunt who acted as a doctor in the community and had a huge influence on Crumpler.
  • 1852- She moved to Charlestown, Massachusetts and worked as a nurse.
  • 1860- She was accepted into the the New England Female Medical College in Boston.
  • 1864- Rebecca graduated from the New England Female Medical College where she was the only African-American graduate. She was also the first African-American woman to become a formally-trained physician.
  • 1864- She began her medical practice in Boston and married Arthur Crumpler. Her care was dedicated primarily to women and children.
  • 1865- Rebecca moved to Richmond, Virginia. "The proper field for real missionary work, and one that would present ample opportunities to become acquainted with the diseases of women and children." She found. However,in Richmond, she experienced racism and sexism from her colleagues, administrators, pharmacists and many others.
  • 1869- She returned to Boston and continued practicing medicine and treated children.
  • 1870s- She attended West Newton English and Classical School in Massachusetts. She also taught in Wilmington beginning in 1874 and in New Castle, Delaware beginning in 1876.
  • 1880-The Crumplers moved to Hyde Park, New York.
  • 1883- Her book, A Book of Medical Discourses was published. Filling with notes Rebecca kept ver the course of her medical career, the book focused on the medical care of women and children and was dedicated to the mothers and nurses. The text and authentication were based on her own which was not common for African-American authors at the time.
  • 1895- Rebecca died in Fairview, Massachusetts, while still residing in Hyde Park.
